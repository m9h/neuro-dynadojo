Robustness to sensor noise — the ground truth that survives

Sensor condition (leak 0.8), sweeping measurement SNR from clean to 0 dB. The wavenumber is flat at ceiling — a global spatial-phase property, immune to both leakage and noise — while directed DMD and undirected correlation decline toward chance.

What the matrix says

shiftsThe identifiable ground truth and the winning method both move with the regime: correlation reads out adjacency under amplitude/phase coupling, but a traveling wave hands that job to partial correlation (conditioning out the global wave), adds directed flow (DMD, Granger), and makes the wavenumber trivially readable.
directionDirectionality is where lag information finally pays: on the wave, DMD .96 and Granger .93 recover the flow that undirected FC (imag-coherence, wPLI ≈ chance) could not.
leakageRobustness to leakage and sensor noise depends on the ground truth, not the method label: the wavenumber is immune (1.00 at every SNR), correlation degrades (.92 → .69), and directed recovery falls hardest — Granger .93 → .46 (zero-lag mixing destroys it), DMD .96 → .57. The global spatial-phase readout is the one that survives a real recording.

Netsim confound battery

Smith et al. (2011)'s confound set, ported to a directed modular Hopf network — edge-recovery AUC for each method as confounds are added one at a time, then all stacked. Two netsim signatures fall out: a shared global input collapses directed recovery (Granger, DMD) while partial correlation still finds the undirected edges; and in the realistic row every method drops to chance because the confounds interact — volume conduction defeats partial correlation's immunity to shared input.

Scaling laws

DynaDojo diagnostics at a moderate operating point (leak 0.4, 10 dB): how recovery scales with system size, data, and difficulty. Recovery falls as nodes grow (more edges, same data), rises and saturates with duration, and climbs with SNR.
